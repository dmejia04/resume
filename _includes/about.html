A Little Bit About Me

Hello, I am Daniel Mejia a 33 years old French/Colombian researcher. I was born and raised in Armenia, a small city in the hart of the Colombian coffee region. I lived there until completed a scientific baccalaureate in 2001. In 2002 I moved to Bogota D.C to continue my education in a aeronautical engineering school. Here I developed a taste for fluid mechanics and propulsion. In the mean time I alternate my studies with work experiences in the US. I spend four winters in the Aspen mountains working as a snowboard guide and a cook. In 2007 I obtained my aeronautical engineering degree from the University of San Beanaventua. In 2008 I moved to Toulouse France to do a masters of science in fluid dynamics at the ISAE-SUPAERO. I graduated in the spring 2010 and I started a Ph.D in fluid mechanics, specialized in combustion physics at IMFT lab in Toulouse. I defended my Ph.D in the spring of 2014. Since then I have been working as a postdoc for the CNRS in the INTECOCIS project. I obtained the french nationality in the winter of 2015.
